About color blindness and its correction

Frequently asked questions

WHAT IS COLOR BLINDNESS?›

Color blindness is a reduced ability to distinguish certain shades, most commonly red and green tones. It is usually inherited and affects how color signals are interpreted by cone cells in the retina.

WHAT DOES IT MEAN, DIAGNOSIS: PROTAN OR DEUTAN COLOR BLINDNESS?›

Protan indicates reduced sensitivity to red light, while deutan indicates reduced sensitivity to green light. These are the two most common forms of red-green color vision deficiency.

WHY COLOR BLINDNESS CORRECTION IS IMPORTANT?›

Correction tools and awareness strategies can improve safety, accessibility, and confidence in everyday tasks such as reading charts, identifying signals, and choosing matching colors.

WHAT IS BACKGROUND OF COLOR BLINDNESS?›

Most cases are genetic and linked to the X chromosome, which is why it is more common in males. It can also be acquired due to eye disease, aging, medication effects, or nerve damage.

CAN COLOR DEFICIENCY OR COLOR BLINDNESS BE CURED?›

Inherited color blindness has no permanent cure today, but supportive options like color filters, adaptive apps, and visual design adjustments can significantly improve daily function.

WHAT ARE THE CHANCES THAT MY SON WILL BE COLOR BLIND IF I AM COLOR BLIND?›

Risk depends on whether the mother carries the gene variant. In X-linked inheritance patterns, sons receive their X chromosome from their mother, so maternal genetics strongly influence the probability.

HOW DO COLORBLIND PEOPLE SEE?›

They usually see a full visual scene but with certain color ranges appearing less distinct. For example, reds and greens may look similar in shade or brightness.

CAN MY COLOR BLINDNESS CHANGE OVER THE YEARS?›

Inherited forms are usually stable, but acquired color vision issues can change over time. Any sudden or progressive change should be evaluated by an eye-care professional.
